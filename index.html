    • About



    Avinoam Lourie (May 28, 1939) is an Israeli zoologist, biologist and prisoner restorer who managed the Haifa Zoo and Wildlife Park from 1970 to 2004. Avinoam defined dozens of fish species, among them a subspecies of Epinefelus tauvina that crossed from the Red Sea to the Mediterranean Sea through the Suez Canal. Avinoam was involved in the development and design of zoos in Israel and was involved in many areas of volunteering throughout his life.

    Avinoam was born in 1939 in the Kiryat Moshe neighborhood of Jerusalem. His father Isidor was a construction engineer at the Ministry of Housing and his beloved mother was a kindergarten teacher. As a child he was known as an animal lover, his first job being at the age of 12 when he would supply venomous snakes to researchers at the Hebrew University. He was drafted into the Armored Corps in the summer of 1957 in the 82nd Battalion of the 7th Brigade as a tank gunner. Avinoam holds a B.Sc. and M.Sc. (laude) in Biology from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem. He is married to Hannah and they have three children. Avinoam received the President's Award for Volunteerism in 1981 from President Yitzhak Navon.

    Since 1975, as part of his work at the Haifa Rotary Club, he has worked in prisoner rehabilitation, prison visits and helped those released to find work. During his tenure as president of the Haifa Rotary, the club raised $100,000 to build a computer center for Ethiopian immigrants. Currently focuses on working with children with special needs in the use of animals.

    An investigator of animal-related crime cases, one of the few people in Israel who can identify the hairs of animals found on poachers and know exactly which animal they hunted. Avinoam retired in 2005 and currently lives in Haifa with his wife Chana. Avinoam published a book in 2010, A Bear in My Bed & A Jackal in My Oven, which tells short stories from his life dealing with animals, family and everything in between. His second book was published in 2017, Hug Dov.
